Signs of a Clogged Filter in the Automatic Transmission and Low Oil Level

Transmission fluid is considered one of the most important components of the automatic transmission (AT) system, serving as the bonding element for the components within the transmission. The oil level, its quality, and the condition of the filter directly affect the functionality of the AT. A low level of ATF and a clogged filter will inevitably lead to breakdowns and, as a result, expensive AT repairs. Poor-quality oil or its shortage reduces pressure in the hydraulic circuits, which in turn leads to:

  • Increased load on the oil pump;
  • Friction discs burning;
  • Increased operating temperature to critical levels;
  • Oil starvation and rapid wear of mechanical parts in the transmission;
  • Clogging of the hydraulic plate and solenoids;
  • Airing of the hydraulic system.

All the components of the AT are interconnected. If one part breaks down, it quickly spreads, affecting other parts. As a result, the AT can fail and become irreparable, or its repair may cost more than replacing the entire unit.

To avoid this, it is necessary to monitor the oil level and quality, as well as to replace filters in a timely manner.

Why Does the Filter Get Clogged and Can This Be Avoided?

Filter clogging is a natural process that occurs in any AT system, regardless of its type or modification. During the operation of the automatic transmission, its mechanical parts rub against each other, producing metal and plastic shavings. These particles enter the oil and remain in it until they encounter the filter. The filter allows ATF to pass through while trapping any debris. Thus, the filter gradually becomes clogged. The speed at which critical clogging occurs depends on the frequency of vehicle use and the temperature in the AT system. Nevertheless, sooner or later, the filter will clog and need to be replaced. If the filter is not replaced, the pressure from the debris will damage the filter, allowing the debris to enter the oil, which will then spread throughout the entire transmission system. The consequences will be critical.

It is impossible to prevent filter clogging, but breakdowns can be avoided. To do this, you need to regularly change the oil and filter, and also respond to the first signs of malfunction by visiting a service center.

Symptoms of a Clogged Filter in the AT

It is very difficult to visually assess the condition of the filter on your own, as this usually requires disassembling the transmission. Therefore, it is better to rely on the symptoms of a clogged filter:

  • When shifting gears, the usual jolt is absent, and the car does not move forward or backward;
  • A jolt is felt during shifting, but there is slipping, or the car still does not move;
  • There is a sensation of sticking when shifting to higher gears;
  • There is slipping of the clutches when shifting gears.

Note that these symptoms are typical of AT malfunctions and may indicate not only a clogged filter but also other problems. Therefore, at the first signs of malfunction, it is better to immediately visit a specialized service center for diagnostics and further filter replacement.

How the Replacement is Done

Before replacing the filter, the technician determines its type. Depending on the type and age of the AT, it may be a coarse filter made of metal or plastic, a fine filter made of paper or synthetic material, and may also come with installed magnets that trap metal particles. Filters are divided into internal and external. Internal filters are located within the AT casing, and to replace them, the transmission must be removed and disassembled. External filters are located outside and filter the ATF circulating through the cooling system.

After determining the type of filter, the material it is made of, and its location, the replacement process is carried out:

  • Disassembly of the AT casing;
  • Removal of the plates covering the filter;
  • Removal of the old filter and installation of the new one;
  • Reassembly of the transmission and reinstallation with all hoses and fasteners connected.

It is important to note that if the filter is made of paper or synthetic materials, it is considered disposable and must be replaced with a new one. However, if it is metallic and there are no cracks or holes on its surface, the filter can be cleaned using special compounds and reinstalled. In any case, the work should be performed by professionals. We do not recommend performing the work yourself to avoid mistakes, even if the filter is located outside the AT.

The filter should be replaced or cleaned every time the oil in the AT is changed. Without this, the replacement is meaningless, as dirty oil will quickly clog the filter again and require another filter change. It is also recommended to replace the oil pan gasket to avoid compromising the system's tightness.

Which Filter is Best for Replacement?

Of course, the best option for installing a filter is the original one. However, they are quite expensive and may not always be available in stores. Therefore, when choosing a filter, focus on the balance between price and quality. Do not save on this component, as a low price may indicate low quality. As a result, you may have to visit the service center again due to the rapid wear of the filter element.

If you don't know which filter to buy, consult with a service technician. They will help you choose, based on the type of transmission and the manufacturer's recommendations.

Symptoms of Low Oil Level in the AT

Low oil in the AT is a very common problem. It occurs due to ATF leakage through the radiator or worn rubber seals that have cracked.

You can determine the oil level yourself using the dipstick that comes with the kit. To do this, insert the dipstick for 5 seconds and check the level and color of the oil by placing it on a clean white cloth or paper. The dipstick has markings to provide the necessary information. Note that the oil should be checked only when hot.

However, not all ATs allow for self-checking of the oil. If there is already a critical shortage, the following symptoms will indicate low oil in the AT:

  • Decreased oil pressure, indicated by the check engine light on the dashboard;
  • Jerks, knocks, or banging sounds during the operation of the transmission;
  • A noticeable decrease in vehicle dynamics, with an increased time to accelerate;
  • Increased average operating temperature of the oil;
  • Oil leakage traces under the vehicle after standing still.

Oil shortage will inevitably lead to oil starvation. The result is accelerated wear of the transmission components due to lack of lubrication. The metal components will rub intensely against each other, leading to AT failure. Therefore, we recommend regularly monitoring the oil level and quality.

If you can check the oil level yourself, do it at least once every 6 months, depending on how often the vehicle is used. It is recommended to change the oil every 40-60 thousand kilometers. If the symptoms of low oil in the AT appear earlier, it is important to contact specialists immediately.

Should You Service Non-Serviceable ATs?

The difference between serviceable and non-serviceable ATs lies only in the frequency of maintenance. Therefore, all types of transmissions require oil changes. The only difference is the frequency. If mineral oil is used in the system, the oil change should occur every 30 thousand kilometers. Semi-synthetic oils are changed every 40-60 thousand km, while synthetic oils are changed every 90 thousand km.

In new ATs, the manufacturer states that the oil is filled for the entire service life. Note that the service life refers to no more than 5 years. However, even within this period, oil changes may be required due to improper driving practices.

Oil and Filter Replacement as the Foundation for Proper AT Operation

Preventing filter and oil clogging is impossible, so it is crucial to respond promptly to symptoms or vehicle mileage. Low oil levels in the AT and symptoms of a clogged filter signal the urgency of visiting a service center. Do not neglect this, as more serious breakdowns caused by oil starvation and clogged filters will soon follow.

We recommend regularly monitoring the oil level and quality, performing preventive diagnostics every 6-12 months, and changing the filter and transmission oil whenever necessary.
