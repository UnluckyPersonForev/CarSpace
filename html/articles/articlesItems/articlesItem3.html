The automatic transmission jerks: why is the gearbox jerking?

Since the automatic transmission became a favorite of car manufacturers and is installed in most modern cars, this component has made driving much more convenient. Despite the wide variety of transmission types, the classic automatic transmission remains one of the most reliable. However, it is also prone to failure for various reasons. Gearbox jerks are one of the most common symptoms of failure. There are many reasons for jerks to occur. Why does the gearbox jerk and how to fix it, we will discuss in this article.

What does “automatic transmission jerks” mean?

Car drivers may encounter such a problem as a delay in shifting gears when moving from one gear to another. Sometimes, when shifting, the car begins to jerk noticeably. In automotive slang, such jerking is called jerks, jolts, or bumps.

For novice drivers, it can be difficult to identify transmission jerks, and they confuse them with engine problems. However, experienced drivers distinguish jerks by several categories:

  • strong, very noticeable jerks when shifting from P to R or D;
  • during driving, there are jerks similar to hitting a bumper, after which the car suddenly accelerates forward;
  • noticeable jolts or hits when shifting from one gear to another.

Before addressing the problem of transmission jerks, it is important to understand the cause of their occurrence.

Why does the automatic transmission jerk?

Gearbox jerks seriously affect comfort or may become a symptom of complete car failure. In practice, jerks are noticeable in the following situations:

  • while driving, when shifting to a higher or lower gear, jerks are felt in the rear bumper, after which the car suddenly moves forward;
  • when moving the selector from P to D or other modes, a noticeable jerk or bump is felt.

There are many possible causes of jerks. They may appear only when the transmission is hot or cold. In any case, it is important not to delay addressing this issue and to consult a professional at a service center for diagnostics.

Only an experienced specialist can pinpoint the exact cause of the jerks. It’s worth mentioning that there are many components whose failure could cause jerks. Let's take a closer look at them.

Engine and transmission mounts

Severe wear on the engine and transmission mounts creates noticeable jolts when shifting into forward or reverse gears. Therefore, when transitioning from the neutral position to drive with the car stationary, if a jerk is felt, it is essential to first check the condition of the engine and transmission mounts.

Engine sensors

Jerks may be caused by faulty engine control sensors. For instance, the mass air flow sensor or throttle position sensor may be the cause of the jerks. Even the Check Engine light may not turn on, and the error may not be recorded in the system. If an error is recorded, it may not correspond to the actual issue—everything could be fine with the parts of the transmission that the error refers to.

Electronic control unit (ECU)

Issues with the ECU are a common cause of jerks. In this case, the transmission may jerk during braking or starting, or at any other time. An electronic diagnostic check of the ECU should be performed or the car’s ECU should be swapped for a known working unit to check the system.

Internal wiring harness

When performing major repairs on certain transmission models, the wiring harness is replaced by default, because worn parts can cause a multitude of transmission issues, including noticeable jerks.

Solenoids

Failure or natural wear of solenoids is one of the most frequent causes of jerks in the automatic transmission. Often, the issue is with the pressure control valve, which needs to be replaced to resolve the symptoms.

Hydraulic block

Jerks may occur due to a malfunction in the hydraulic valve plate of the transmission. The cause could be wear on the valve seats (mounting surfaces) or deformation of the hydraulic block caused by overheating of the transmission. This leads to a drop in oil pressure, which in turn causes jerks.

Mechatronic unit

If the solenoids of your car’s automatic transmission are controlled by a separate unit (mechatronic), the first thing to check if jerks occur is the condition of the mechatronic unit. Its failure is often the cause of jerking.

Mechanical parts

Jerks can also occur due to wear of the mechanical parts of the automatic transmission. Failures of the band brakes, friction clutch packs, and other components of the planetary gear sets can lead to a variety of symptoms of failure.

Radiator problems

The transmission system is very sensitive to temperature. Therefore, a functioning radiator and optimal temperature control are crucial for stable operation. If the oil becomes overheated, jerks will soon follow.

It is essential to maintain the required temperature range for the transmission system and avoid critical overheating. We recommend timely repairs to the transmission radiator to prevent serious transmission issues.

Oil issues

This issue is considered the most common, and we will discuss it in detail. Transmission fluid is a vital element in the operation of the automatic transmission. It is the key working fluid in the transmission because, unlike manual transmissions, the automatic transmission does not have a direct mechanical connection to the engine, and the fluid serves as the link in the torque converter responsible for engagement. Therefore, when jerks or bumps occur, the first thing the technician will do is check the oil. Any deviation in its quantity or quality can lead to jerking.

The oil operates under high temperatures, and over time, it loses its valuable properties due to this. As a result, it becomes ineffective at transmitting torque from the engine to the transmission, becoming either too thick or too thin. The result is jerks or bumps. This is why it is crucial to change the transmission fluid on time, as we previously discussed in this article.

When changing the fluid, the oil filter should also be replaced, as it gets clogged over time and no longer allows oil to pass through in the proper quantity. This can cause transmission issues and jerks when shifting gears.

It is very important to use the correct amount of oil when replacing it and to use only the transmission fluid recommended by the manufacturer. Sometimes, certain brands of oil recommended by the car manufacturer may be difficult or impossible to find in Ukraine. In such cases, a high-quality universal oil suitable for your transmission should be chosen. Using unsuitable or low-quality oil can lead to serious failures, and jerks or bumps will become just a minor issue.

What to do if the transmission jerks?

First of all, go to a service station. Timely identification of the malfunction, the symptom of which are jerks or bumps, will help avoid costly repairs. First, a diagnostic check is needed. The diagnosis includes:

  • A full analysis of the transmission fluid: quantity, viscosity level, color, presence of debris or dirt;
  • Checking the electronic control unit for errors. If errors are found, the technician will decode them;
  • Pressure measurement, determining the wear level of the solenoids;
  • Checking the GDT lock and other components during vehicle movement.

Based on the diagnostic results, the technician will determine the cause of the jerks and suggest repair options.

What to do if the bumps during gear shifting appear only when cold?

Many car owners often complain that they feel bumps when the gearbox is cold and not warmed up. In fact, this does not always indicate a malfunction in the automatic transmission. Most often, the jerks occur due to simple circumstances that are quickly eliminated.

The reasons for jerks in a cold transmission are:

  • Poor quality or old ATF;
  • Low oil level;
  • Insufficient warm-up of the automatic transmission components.

The solution in these cases is straightforward. First, warm up the transmission. It is not recommended to start driving with a cold gearbox, as oil thickens at low temperatures and does not provide maximum efficiency. If the problem persists and the bumps remain, check the oil and, if necessary, top it up to the recommended level or replace it if the transmission fluid is in poor condition.

What to do if the transmission jerks when hot?

If the bumps appear only with a hot transmission, a diagnosis is required to identify the cause. As mentioned earlier, there can be many reasons. First, the cause may still lie with the oil. If the oil is fine, the next step is to check the cooling system. After that, all transmission components should be analyzed. The cause of bumps on a hot transmission may be a malfunction of any part, and only an experienced technician can identify it after a thorough diagnosis.

What to do if the transmission jerks only when braking?

Sometimes bumps appear in the transmission when the brake is pressed. This is often related to a software malfunction in the unit.

It is impossible to fix this problem on your own, and it is not recommended to try. To solve the software issue, a reprogramming is required, and doing this yourself is very dangerous and requires experience. As practice shows, new transmissions often require a software update. This not only eliminates the bumps but also optimizes the operation of the unit. Full computer diagnostics should be carried out before reprogramming.

What to do if the jerks appear only during gear shifting?

It may also happen that the transmission jerks only when shifting gears. Most often, this occurs due to poor quality, dirty oil, or insufficient oil in the system. If the jerks are felt only when shifting into reverse, the reasons may be:

  • Problems with the controller - dirty contacts or damaged connectors;
  • Driving with a cold gearbox;
  • Problems with the oil.

If warming up the gearbox and changing the oil doesn't help, a transmission diagnosis is required to find the malfunction that causes the jerks and bumps as a symptom.

How dangerous are transmission jerks?

As we can see, there are many causes for bumps and jerks. They often become a symptom of a malfunction that should be addressed immediately. However, there are situations where bumps can be eliminated by simply warming up the transmission.

In any case, each transmission and each situation should be treated individually, as factors such as the type of transmission, the type of oil used, the frequency of vehicle use, its age, and much more matter.

Transmission diagnostics is the main and primary method of determining the malfunction that caused the symptom of bumps or jerks. Afterward, repairs or part replacements should be carried out, depending on which component has failed.

In any case, do not delay addressing such symptoms. Contact professionals immediately to extend the lifespan of your vehicle's transmission and avoid spending large amounts of money on repairs.
