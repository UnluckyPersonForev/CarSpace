Manual transmission, automatic transmission, CVT or robot. What to choose? Which is better?

For those planning to buy a car, the task is not easy. The huge variety of makes and models in different price ranges can be overwhelming. Especially if the future car owner is not familiar with the technology. The choice of car depends on the budget and preferences. But the main selection criterion is often the type of transmission. Nowadays, cars are produced with different types of transmissions. Each of them has its advantages and disadvantages, operating features, and maintenance costs. So, what should you choose? This is what our article is about.

Types of Gearboxes

First, let's clarify that you shouldn't choose a car solely based on the type of gearbox. At the very least, because every driver has their own preferences and opinions. It's also worth noting that every manufacturer, in their history of producing cars, has had “flaws,” releasing gearboxes with errors and shortcomings. We're not talking about Chinese cars. Brands like Toyota, Mitsubishi, and Volkswagen have been found with notable issues.

Therefore, the choice of gearbox should be based on rationality. It's necessary to gather detailed information about the specific model and type of gearbox. For example, what are the most common failures with this gearbox, whether there are problems right from the factory, what potential defects exist, and if there are any weak spots. Collecting this detailed information will help you choose the most optimal option based on driving experience and other subjective factors.

Also, don't forget that nowadays, with fierce competition between automakers, everyone is trying to pull the blanket towards themselves. For example, the VAG group, which produces cars under the Audi, Volkswagen, and Škoda brands, started installing their version of the gearbox called DSG. In fact, DSG is just a regular robot with dual clutches. But the new name distinguishes the manufacturer and confuses the buyer. However, this gearbox has its own advantages and features. It consists of two units. One of them is responsible for 1st, 3rd, and 5th gears, while the other is responsible for 2nd, 4th, and 6th. The operation is fully automatic, with high-quality electronics involved. Therefore, the driver does nothing. When the gearbox shifts to 2nd gear, the next step is the automatic shift to 3rd gear. It is in standby mode and shifts automatically from 2nd to 3rd. And it does so in milliseconds. The driver doesn't even notice it. Acceleration is smooth and pleasant. There are no jolts or jerks. This type of gearbox is ideal for those who enjoy agile, fast driving.

There is also another type of gearbox called Powershift. These are installed in Ford vehicles. The Powershift gearbox operates similarly to DSG. The difference lies in the software and settings. The similarity between the two types of gearboxes is not only in the operating principle but also in the components. Their quantity and types are practically the same, but they are produced by different manufacturers.

In the end, all the existing types of gearboxes can be divided into four types:

  • Manual Transmission (MT);
  • Automatic Transmission (AT);
  • Robotized Transmission;
  • Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT).

Each type has its characteristics and fans. Some claim that AT is the only possible best option. Others prefer the CVT. We'll discuss each type of gearbox below.

Manual Transmission (MT)

Worldwide, the lion's share of car sales is accounted for by manual transmissions. In some countries, most cars are produced only with manual gearboxes. In the CIS countries, where a wide range of cars with different types of transmissions is available, some car enthusiasts prefer the MT.

This is likely because this type of gearbox is the cheapest. For every car enthusiast, their first car was likely a manual transmission, and it's the type they learned to drive on. Many are convinced that a manual is durable, indestructible, and cheap to maintain. Partially, this is true. But only partially.

The issue is that there is a wide range of manual gearboxes, many of which have more drawbacks than even the most unreliable automatics. Therefore, when choosing an MT, you need to consider the manufacturer and its reputation. However, even then, there is always the risk of buying a car with an unreliable MT.

So, which gearbox should you choose?

We can't definitively nominate a winner for the "best gearbox" award. Every car enthusiast has their own criteria for selection. If practicality, reliability, and long-term operation are important, then manual transmission is unbeaten. If comfort is the priority – choose an automatic transmission based on a torque converter. CVTs are slowly becoming a serious competitor to both manual and automatic transmissions, offering incredible dynamics, smoothness, and driving pleasure. However, manufacturers still can't extend the lifespan of this gearbox. The robotized gearbox provides maximum driving comfort and is truly smart and adapted to all conditions. But it remains the least reliable.

In this article, we’ve highlighted the pros and cons of each option. Assess them and make your choice, the one that suits you best.
