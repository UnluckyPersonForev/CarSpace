Automatic Transmission (AT) Diagnostics

Before any repair begins, automatic transmission diagnostics are performed. This is a mandatory and essential part of the service process. Transmission diagnostics help determine the overall condition of the gearbox, the quality, color, and consistency of the oil, the condition of components critical to the transmission's operation, as well as identify the type and cause of the malfunction. Diagnostic work starts with a test drive to identify any symptoms of failure, followed by a visual inspection of external components (such as the transmission pan and the presence of metal shavings inside it). If the issue is internal, the technician removes the transmission and performs a teardown (defect analysis). During this process, all transmission components are inspected for integrity and potential damage. Only after diagnostics are completed can the malfunction and the appropriate method of repair be determined.

The Benefits of Our Automatic Transmission Repair Service

The first stage of transmission repair is vehicle intake and diagnostics. This is carried out in several steps: test drive, visual inspection, computer diagnostics, and defect analysis (optional, if needed). After diagnostics, once the issue is identified and the repair timeline and cost are approved by the client, the technician proceeds with the service. The transmission is removed in accordance with the vehicle manufacturer's technical guidelines. Components are cleaned with a special solution, and the cooling system is flushed. Repairs are carried out with replacement of parts (if necessary) or restoration of existing ones. Transmission fluid and filters are replaced if required. The transmission is then reassembled and reinstalled. Finally, transmission adaptation is performed, followed by a final test drive to ensure the issue has been resolved and to assess all key performance indicators of the system.
