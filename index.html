    All Types of Breakdowns

    We can fix any transmission problem, even if you've broken it in half! No matter what caused the malfunction: failure or wear of mechanical, hydraulic or electronic components. Often a problem in one part of the transmission leads to failure in another, so the sooner you visit a service center, the less the repair will cost.

    Complete Auto Service
    Car owners typically recognize automatic transmission (AT) failures through specific symptoms. Most often, the malfunction is displayed as an error code by the onboard computer that monitors the transmission's technical parameters. In addition to dashboard warning lights, common symptoms include:
    Jerks or jolts from the automatic transmission during vehicle start or gear changes;
    Gear slipping, sticking, or complete disengagement of one or more gears; An unusual burning smell or odor of melted rubber/plastic from the transmission;
    Fluid leaks (oil spots on the ground under the vehicle);
    Complete vehicle failure. Even minor symptoms or any unusual vehicle behavior indicate the need for immediate professional automatic transmission repair. Remember, timely maintenance, repairs, or part replacements can save on labor costs and preserve your transmission without major overhauls.
